AI Governance & Privacy Engineering: Data Subject Deletion Rights

Analyzed privacy engineering concepts through critical evaluation of LLM-generated responses across topics including data governance, privacy frameworks, and regulatory compliance.

Conducted a focused analysis on data subject deletion rights across GDPR, PIPEDA, and CCPA examining implementation challenges, system architectures, cryptographic deletion mechanisms, and cross-jurisdictional compliance considerations.

Systematically evaluated AI outputs (GPT-4o mini) by identifying inaccuracies, omissions, and gaps in legal nuance. Validated findings using primary legal texts and peer-reviewed academic references.

Includes a presentation highlighting implications for privacy engineering including privacy risks, system design considerations for AI-assisted systems, and the challenge of bridging AI capabilities with real-world privacy and compliance requirements.

Key Topics Covered:

--> Regulatory Comparison — GDPR (Art. 17), CCPA (§1798.105), and PIPEDA (Principle 4.5): deletion grounds, exemptions, timelines, and enforcement mechanisms.

--> System Architectures — Centralized, decentralized, and hybrid approaches for implementing deletion rights at scale; analysis of blockchain's fundamental incompatibility with the right to erasure.

--> Cryptographic Secure Deletion — AES-256 encryption at rest, hierarchical key management (DEK/KMS/HSM), and key destruction as a scalable deletion mechanism.

--> Implementation Challenges — Data mapping across distributed systems, backup deletion strategy (GDPR Recital 65), accidental deletion mitigation, and legal retention conflicts.

--> IAB Data Deletion Request Framework (DDRF) — JWT-based protocol (idJWT, rqJWT, acJWT), JWKS endpoint discovery via dsrdelete.json, and 8-step data flow across publishers and vendors.

--> LLM Evaluation Methodology — Structured annotation framework categorizing AI responses as Correct / Not Nuanced / Incorrect / Irrelevant / Repeated / IDK
